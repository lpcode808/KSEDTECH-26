# CONNECT26 Conference Guide — Usability Assessment

> **Note:** This assessment was generated by an AI agent. All three personas, their quoted observations, and the walkthrough narratives are **synthetic** — they do not represent real people or real user-testing sessions. The usability findings are grounded in a full code read of the app and reflect genuine issues in the implementation, but no actual users were observed.

**Date:** 2026-05-29
**Scope:** The root `index.html` conference companion app (Schedule / Speakers / Notes), served at `https://lpcode808.github.io/KSEDTECH-26/`.
**Method:** Three attendee personas walk the app end-to-end *at the conference* (not pre-registration browsing), take notes, then meet to reconcile and prioritize. Findings are grounded in a full read of the app's data and logic.

---

## The app at a glance

- **72 schedule entries**: 19 confirmed timed blocks (keynotes, meals, breakouts) + 53 untimed "Session Catalog" rows (`time: "TBD"`, `location: "Breakout rooms TBD"`).
- **62 speakers**, searchable by name / role / company; can be **starred** (localStorage), swipe-to-star on mobile, "show starred up top," clear-all.
- **5 learning strands**: Culture, Transformation, Well-being, Advocacy, Agency — with a working pill filter above the schedule.
- **Notes**: per-session, per-speaker, and free "quick" notes; localStorage; export to text/markdown/JSON; import with merge.
- **Offline-first** intent via service worker; QR "share this app" modal.
- Strong baseline a11y: roles, `aria-expanded`/`aria-controls`, `:focus-visible` rings, live regions, 44px touch targets.

## Persona 1 — Kumu Mahina Kealoha

> Hawaiian-language & culture-based learning teacher, ~50s. Older iPhone, mostly one-handed. Spotty venue Wi-Fi. Tech comfort: moderate. **Goal:** find the place-based / Culture sessions and the people behind them; trust that ʻōlelo Hawaiʻi renders correctly.

**Walkthrough notes**
- ʻOkina/kahakō render correctly in titles (`ʻĀina Ulu Huakaʻi`, `ʻEnehana`). 👍 Trust earned immediately.
- Taps **Culture** in the strand filter — list narrows, good. But several sessions she *knows* are culture-centered (the "all-strands" community sessions) **don't carry a Culture chip and aren't pulled in by the filter**. She assumes they're not relevant and skips them. ⚠️
- The "All" pill says **43**, but the heads-up banner promises a **55-session catalog**. The mismatch makes her wonder what she's missing. ⚠️
- Loses her place in the long list — every catalog row shows the same **"TBD"** in the time column, so the page reads as one undifferentiated wall. Hard to tell pre-conference / Day 1 / Day 2 / catalog apart while thumb-scrolling. ⚠️
- Opens the **Share this app** QR on the show floor with no signal — the code **fails to load** (it's a remote image). The app sells itself as offline-first, so this surprises her. ⚠️
- Likes the per-speaker "🏢 organization" line; wishes she could tell at a glance who is *Kamehameha / DOE / community* vs. mainland vendor.

## Persona 2 — Jordan Park

> Instructional coach & self-described power user, ~30s. Android phone + laptop. Tech comfort: high. **Goal:** build a tight personal plan across 3 days, capture takeaways per session, and **export notes to share with the team** afterward.

**Walkthrough notes**
- Stars favorite **speakers** quickly (swipe + "show starred up top" is excellent). Then hits the wall: **you cannot star a session.** His whole planning model is "which *sessions* am I attending," and the app only lets him star people. ⚠️ (Top frustration.)
- Works around it by leaving a one-word note on each session he wants — but notes are for content, not a plan; there's no "my picks" view to scan.
- **Export to markdown** is genuinely great — clean headings, timestamps, round-trip JSON import with merge. This is the feature he'll evangelize.
- Search is fast and matches title/desc/speaker/strand/location. But it **doesn't search the plain-language summaries**, and speaker search doesn't match the *titles of sessions a person is presenting* — so "who's doing the AI assessment talk" via the Speakers tab misses.
- Strand filter + search compose correctly. 👍
- Notices the **"Who's In The Room"** affordance never appears (all `subItems` empty) — dead weight in the code, not user-facing.

## Persona 3 — Maile Reyes

> First-time attendee, early-career classroom teacher, ~20s. Mobile only, walking between rooms. Tech comfort: moderate. **Goal:** reduce overwhelm — "where do I go next and is it worth my time?"

**Walkthrough notes**
- Opens to the Schedule tab mid-conference and is immediately overwhelmed: a flat list of 72 items with no day separators and no "happening now." She can't quickly answer "what's next." ⚠️ (Top frustration.)
- The "TBD" times make the catalog feel unfinished and erode trust ("is this schedule even real?").
- The **plain-language summary** (🤖) is the feature she loves most — it tells her *why* a session matters in plain words. But it's hidden one tap deep and only exists for the timed blocks; the 53 catalog rows have **no summary** (the toggle is correctly suppressed, but she's left with dense vendor-ish descriptions instead).
- Tapping a speaker chip jumps her to the speaker — nice — but getting *back* to where she was in the schedule means a long re-scroll.
- Smooth-scroll animations are pleasant; she has motion sensitivity and there's **no reduced-motion accommodation**, so the auto-scroll-into-view is slightly nauseating after a few jumps. ⚠️

## The personas compare notes (synthesis)

**Where they violently agree**
1. **The schedule needs structure.** Maile (can't orient) and Mahina (loses her place) both fail at the same root cause: a flat 72-row list with "TBD" noise and no day grouping or "now" anchor. *This is the single highest-leverage fix.*
2. **Strand filtering is half-true.** Mahina is actively misled and Jordan distrusts the "43 vs 55" count. The multi-strand sessions silently fall out of the headline feature.
3. **Planning is people-first, but attendees think session-first.** Jordan wants to star sessions; Maile wants a "my next stop" list. The starring machinery already exists for speakers — the pattern is proven, it just isn't applied to sessions.

**Where they differ (and how we resolved it)**
- Jordan wanted richer search (summaries, cross-tab). Maile/Mahina didn't care. → *Nice-to-have, not top tier.*
- Mahina wanted org/affiliation badges; Jordan wanted "who's in the room." → *Lower priority; one is editorial data work, one is dead code.*
- All three independently flagged the **offline QR** breaking — it's small but it directly contradicts the app's offline promise.

## Implementation notes (this pass)

- **Multi-strand**: the lift step now parses both `Official CONNECT strand: X` and `Official CONNECT strands: A, B, C` into `s.strands` (array). Chips render per strand; the filter keeps a session if its `strands` includes the active strand; counts reflect every catalog row that names a strand.
- **Day grouping**: `renderSchedule()` emits a sticky `.day-group-header` whenever the `day` label changes, preserving source order. Works with strand/search/“My Picks” filtering (empty groups are omitted).
- **TBD**: a `TBD` start time renders as a muted “TBD” affordance rather than masquerading as a clock time; meal/break rows keep their times.
- **Reduced motion**: a global `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` neutralizes transitions/animations and disables smooth scroll; `scrollIntoView` falls back to `auto`.
- **Session stars**: new `connect26_starred_sessions` key with get/toggle/persist/clear mirroring speakers; a star control on each session, a starred indicator, a **★ My Picks** pill in the filter row, and a "Starred Sessions" block in the markdown export.
